Design note · read me first
The material is the brand

Warm ceramic neutrals — bone, unfired clay, wet stoneware, kiln-shadow — keep the UI quiet so pottery photos carry the color. Accents are pulled straight from real glaze chemistry.

Capture is instant, browse is a showpiece

Spectacle (GSAP scroll sequences, staggered reveals, material shimmer) lives on landing & browse. The upload flow is stripped to photo → glaze → done, with motion confined to gentle confirmation.

Studio-first, but themeable

Every screen is built for The Fine Line and its glazes. The system is fully tokenized, so opening it to other studios later is a re-theme, not a rebuild.

◐ GSAP · browse

Cards stagger up on scroll; tapping a piece runs a shared-element transition into the detail photo. Filtering reflows the grid with FLIP so pieces glide to new spots instead of snapping.

◐ Motion · add flow (restrained)

No blocking animation. Chips pop in on select (120ms), the submit is instant, and the only celebration is the check-mark scale on success. Never make a member wait to log a piece.

Reduced motion

With prefers-reduced-motion set, all reveals become instant cross-fades, the spinner becomes a static progress label, and parallax is disabled — the app stays calm and fully usable.

Why passwordless

Studio members span every age and comfort level. A magic link removes the single biggest failure point — forgotten passwords at the kiln — while SSO gives the confident one-tap in. Both paths land in the same warm, trusted home.

Accessibility built in
  • All text meets WCAG AA on its surface (ink #2A2018 on bone = 12:1).
  • Tap targets ≥ 44px throughout; SSO & submit are 48px+.
  • Real <label> on every field incl. the type-ahead.
  • Full keyboard nav; visible focus rings on the warm palette.
  • prefers-reduced-motion degrades every reveal to a fade.
